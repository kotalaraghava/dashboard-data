### Today's Energy

**Surya/Pitta** | Nakshatra: Dhanishtha | Tithi: Purnima, Krishna Paksha

Surya in Vrishabha meets Dhanishtha's swift, dynamic shakti under Krishna Paksha's waning moon — a day for consolidation and inward turning. Establish firm sadhana early; Rahu at sunset marks the boundary between outer action and inner witness. Vata rising from lunar descent requires grounding practices and light, warm meals.

### Seasonal Guidance (Varsa — Monsoon)

Deshacharya active: Surya in Vrishabha (Vasanta base) but Parjanya [rainfall] in Bangalore → following Varsa Ritucharya per Ashtanga Hridayam Sutrasthana Ch.3 | Weather: 🌧️ 30°C · 75% humidity · monsoon

Varsa increases Vata and Pitta in the body. Agni weakens from high moisture; digestive fires require support through warm, well-cooked foods and mild spices (ginger, cumin, black pepper). Avoid raw, cold, heavy foods. Takra (salted buttermilk) aids digestion. Oil massage (abhyanga) becomes protective — the body needs unctuous warmth against damp.

**Top 5 Fruits** *(Varsa Ritucharya — available in Bangalore, support weakened Agni)*

1. **Pomegranate** — Astringent-sweet rasa, balances Vata, strengthens Agni. Classical Varsa choice (Charaka Samhita Su.6.18). Bangalore availability: peak season.
2. **Mango (ripe, not green)** — Sweet, warming (when ripe), supports digestion if taken in moderation. Avoid green (too astringent for monsoon).
3. **Papaya** — Sweet, light, aids weak digestion via enzymes. Charaka recommends for Varsa when taken cooked or as ripened fruit.
4. **Dates** — Sweet, warm, nourishing to Vata. Support energy during the moist, depleting Varsa season.
5. **Grapes (fresh, seeded)** — Sweet, cool but light. Balances Pitta without aggravating Vata if eaten in season.

**Top 5 Vegetables** *(Varsa Ritucharya — well-cooked, available in Bangalore)*

Consider: raw is prohibited in Varsa per Ashtanga Hridayam — all vegetables must be well-cooked with warming spices.

1. **Bitter gourd (karela)** — Bitter-astringent, supports weak Agni, purifies Pitta. Cook well with salt, turmeric, ginger. Bangalore availability: excellent in monsoon.
2. **Ridge gourd (tinda)** — Light, easily digestible, warming when well-cooked with ghee and cumin. Classical Varsa vegetable.
3. **Bottle gourd (lauki)** — Sweet, cooling (balanced by warm spices), aids digestion. Cook with ginger and cumin oil.
4. **Drumstick (moringa) leaves** — Warming, digestive, rich in minerals. Simmer in warm water with turmeric and salt.
5. **Spinach (palak)** — Iron-rich, but cook well with ghee and spices to offset Varsa's dampening effect on digestion.

Wife (3 months pregnant): Varsa increases Vata and Pitta in pregnancy. Keep her warm, well-nourished with cooked foods, ghee, and dates. Avoid raw salads. The waning moon (Krishna Paksha) can increase subtle anxieties — warm oil massage (garbha-specific abhyanga) is grounding. Encourage rest and warm herbal teas.
