### Seasonal Guidance (Varsa — Monsoon)

Surya in Vrishabha (Vasanta base) but Parjanya (rain) active at 76% humidity with drizzle (weathercode 51) → following Varsa Ritucharya per Deshacharya. Vata peaks during monsoon—light, warm, well-digested foods are essential. Agni (digestion) needs support; avoid cold, raw, and heavy foods. Cook all vegetables thoroughly.

**Top 5 Fruits** *(Varsa season — cooling, digestible, light)*

1. **Mango (ripe)** — Sweet, cooling, easy to digest. Supports weak Agni during monsoon; aids postpartum recovery (wife).
2. **Pomegranate** — Astringent, warming slightly, strengthens Agni. Protects against seasonal infections.
3. **Papaya** — Light, aids digestion, cooling. Fresh availability in Bangalore; supports Vata balance.
4. **Coconut (fresh, young)** — Cooling, nourishing liquid, balances high Vata and Pitta in monsoon.
5. **Grapes (black, when available)** — Sweet, slightly cooling, easy to digest. Builds tissues after fasting periods.

**Top 5 Vegetables** *(Varsa — well-cooked only, avoid raw)*

1. **Ridge gourd (Gilki)** — Light, cooling, easily digested. Must be cooked tender; traditional for Varsa.
2. **Bitter gourd** — Cleansing, supports weak digestion. Cook with ghee to balance bitterness.
3. **Bottle gourd (Lauki)** — Very light, cooling, calming to Vata. Ideal for monsoon; always cooked.
4. **Green beans** — Mild, light, with ghee or sesame oil. Avoid raw.
5. **Carrot (cooked in ghee)** — Sweet, nourishing, warming slightly. Supports during Varsa; softens in water.

**Wife (3 months pregnant, Varsa season):** During monsoon with waning moon and Shani's heaviness, prioritize warm, well-cooked, easily digestible foods. Avoid raw salads and cold water. Add Takra (buttermilk) daily for Agni support and Vata balance. Light exercise (walking) supports circulation; avoid heavy lifting during Krishna Paksha.
